\section{Pathway Selection}

All treatment-mediator pairs were evaluated across communities the significant communities, but only $9 \to 11$ and $11 \to 9$ yielded non-degenerate mediation decompositions. All others failed the minimum presence test (fewer than 50 articles tagged with both the treatment and mediator community) and were therefore dropped from analysis.

For the $9 \to 11$ pathway, no annotator produces a significant Total Effect. The only significant cell is the Natural Direct Effect under baseline GPT-4o-mini (NDE $= -0.093$, 95\% CI $[-0.174, -0.002]$): when Community~11 sentiment is held at its $Q_{25}$ level, increasing Community~9 sentiment from $Q_{25}$ to $Q_{75}$ shifts the predicted ideology label leftward in the baseline GPT-4o-mini paradigm, even though the corresponding TE ($-0.064$) does not itself reach significance. Four of the five paradigms estimate a negative Total Effect (Human $-0.045$, Fine-tuned RoBERTa $-0.069$, baseline GPT $-0.064$, Llama-3.3-70B $-0.050$); Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini is the lone directional outlier with TE $= +0.003$, the only paradigm whose point estimate is positive in this pathway. Among the four agreeing paradigms, Fine-tuned RoBERTa carries the largest negative magnitude.

For the $11 \to 9$ pathway, no annotator reaches significance on the Total Effect, but Llama-3.3-70B's Natural Direct Effect crosses the 95\% threshold (NDE $= -0.057$, 95\% CI $[-0.126, -0.003]$), and Llama also carries the largest-magnitude TE ($-0.081$) of any paradigm in this pathway. All five paradigms now estimate a negative Total Effect (Human $-0.045$, Fine-tuned RoBERTa $-0.016$, baseline GPT $-0.027$, Fine-tuned GPT $-0.040$, Llama $-0.081$); the directional consensus in $11 \to 9$ is therefore complete, while the $9 \to 11$ pathway contains a single directional outlier (Fine-tuned GPT). The asymmetry between the two pathways now lies primarily in \emph{which} paradigm carries the significant direct channel (baseline GPT in $9 \to 11$, Llama in $11 \to 9$) and in directional homogeneity (4/5 in $9 \to 11$ vs 5/5 in $11 \to 9$), rather than in whether either pathway is significant on the Total Effect.

\section{Confounder Reduction Details}

Confounders consist of all community sentiment scores except those of the treatment and mediator communities, reduced in two sequential steps:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Presence filter:} Communities whose sentiment is non-zero in fewer than 50 articles in the treatment subsample are dropped.
    \item \textbf{PCA:} The remaining matrix is reduced via PCA retaining 95\% of variance.
\end{enumerate}

Because this reduction depends only on the treatment subsample, not on ideology labels, it is identical across all annotation paradigms for a given treatment community. All treatment communities begin with 11 potential confounders. For Communities 1, 3, and 11, the presence filter removes 3, leaving 8; PCA further reduces these to 7 components. Community~9 loses one additional community (4 removed, leaving 7), and PCA retains all 7. All configurations therefore yield 7 final confounder components; the choice of mediator community has no effect on this reduction.
