\section{Ideology Prediction Distributions}

Figure~\ref{fig:bias_overall_appendix} shows the overall prediction distribution across the full dataset. GPT-4o-mini (baseline) exhibits a moderate Centre bias relative to Human and Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini, Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini and Llama-3.3-70B both skew leftward, and human annotations are the most right-leaning overall, reflecting the ideological composition of the AllSidesV2 corpus. The leftward shift of Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini relative to its zero-shot baseline is best read as a calibration artifact of fine-tuning rather than as a substantive ideological commitment of the model: the AllSidesV2 training distribution is itself imbalanced toward left-leaning articles (roughly 43\% Left versus 34\% Right), and supervised fine-tuning calibrates the model's output prior to that empirical class frequency, dragging its predicted distribution leftward in lockstep. The same mechanism explains why the baseline GPT, which has not been calibrated to AllSidesV2's class prior, instead defaults to a more moderate prediction distribution.

Most communities yield small, statistically non-significant ATEs across all annotation sources, with confidence intervals spanning zero. Given the modest dataset size (11,516 articles, with individual communities containing as few as 95 articles after topic filtering), wide confidence intervals are expected. Two cells reach significance: Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini in Community~3 ($+0.027$, 95\% CI $[+0.001, +0.033]$) and baseline GPT-4o-mini in Community~9 ($-0.015$, 95\% CI $[-0.033, -0.000]$). The relative patterns across annotation sources remain informative even where individual cells are not significant.

Across most communities, the five paradigms agree on the direction of the sentiment-ideology effect; the differences are primarily in magnitude and in which paradigm crosses the significance threshold. In Community~3 (Social Issues), all five paradigms estimate a positive effect (Human $+0.011$, Fine-tuned RoBERTa $+0.001$, baseline GPT $+0.008$, Fine-tuned GPT $+0.027$, Llama $+0.008$); Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini amplifies this shared direction into the only significant cell, in the rightward direction. In Community~9 (Geopolitics and Foreign Affairs), all five paradigms estimate a negative effect with point estimates clustered within $0.002$ of $-0.015$ (Human $-0.014$, Fine-tuned RoBERTa $-0.015$, baseline GPT $-0.015$, Fine-tuned GPT $-0.008$, Llama $-0.014$); only baseline GPT's interval narrowly excludes zero. In Community~11 (Trump Presidency), all five paradigms estimate a negative effect (Human $-0.017$, Fine-tuned RoBERTa $-0.008$, baseline GPT $-0.006$, Fine-tuned GPT $-0.014$, Llama $-0.020$); Llama-3.3-70B carries the largest negative magnitude and Human is second.

Directional disagreement across paradigms is rare in the new run. The clearest case is Community~1 (Electoral Politics), where Fine-tuned RoBERTa is the only paradigm to estimate a positive effect ($+0.001$); the other four paradigms estimate small negative effects. The earlier draft's framing of Community~1 as a tight three-way alignment between Human, baseline GPT, and Fine-tuned GPT no longer holds: baseline GPT ($-0.003$) has flattened toward zero, while Human ($-0.014$) and Fine-tuned GPT ($-0.011$) remain comparable in magnitude.

These results point to two paradigm-specific tendencies that survive in the new data. First, Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini amplifies shared sentiment-ideology directions to a degree that other paradigms do not, sharpening the Community~3 positive effect into the only significant rightward community-level result. Second, Fine-tuned RoBERTa produces uniformly small estimates that track the human direction in Community~9 ($-0.015$ vs Human $-0.014$); the broader claim that RoBERTa preserves a distinctively human-aligned direction across multiple communities is no longer supported, since Communities~3 and~11 are now situations of full-paradigm directional agreement. We do not retain the earlier characterisation of Llama as a uniform attenuator: Llama produces small effects on broadly distributed communities but comparable-to-larger effects on the politically charged Communities~9 and~11, where it carries the largest negative magnitude (Community~11) or sits within rounding distance of the significance threshold (Community~9).
