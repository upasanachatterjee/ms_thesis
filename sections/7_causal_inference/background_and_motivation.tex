\section{Chapter Overview}
\label{sec:causal-background}

As established in Chapter~\ref{sec:introduction}, the correlation between topic-specific sentiment and ideological framing is well documented, but correlation alone cannot tell us whether sentiment \emph{causes} ideology classification or merely co-occurs with it. Standard accuracy metrics such as F1 are equally uninformative: two classifiers can produce identical labels while relying on entirely different internal pathways, one aligned with human reasoning and the other exploiting a spurious shortcut. This chapter applies causal inference methods to the AllSidesV2 Media test set in order to probe these pathways directly.

Section~\ref{sec:causal-methodology} formalizes the causal structure using a directed acyclic graph, defines the variables of interest (text, topic, sentiment, perceived ideology), and describes our Double Machine Learning (DML) methodology, including multi-treatment ATE estimation and mediation analysis. Section~\ref{sec:community-level-results} presents community-level results, where aggregating topic sentiments within detected communities yields the strongest and most interpretable effects: only the fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini produces significant effects, while human, Fine-tuned RoBERTa, and Llama-3.3-70B annotations remain null. Section~\ref{sec:topic-level-results} reports topic-level results for completeness; no robust causal effects emerge at this granularity, significance is paradigm-dependent, and estimated causal directions can reverse depending on the annotation model used. Section~\ref{sec:cross-paradigm-synthesis} synthesizes findings across both analysis levels and all five annotation paradigms, interpreting the fine-tuned GPT divergence as evidence of shortcut learning: fine-tuning introduces spurious sentiment-ideology coupling that is invisible to standard F1-based evaluation but surfaces under causal probing.

All results are reported across five annotation paradigms: human labels, a fine-tuned RoBERTa classifier, zero-shot GPT-4o-mini, fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini, and zero-shot Llama-3.3-70B.
