\section{Cross-Paradigm Synthesis}
\label{sec:cross-paradigm-synthesis}

Having presented topic-level and community-level results separately, we now synthesize findings across both analysis levels and all five annotation paradigms.

\subsection{Agreement Patterns Across Analysis Levels}

At the \textbf{topic level}, no topic sentiment produces a statistically significant effect that is consistent across annotation paradigms. Human and GPT-4o-mini (baseline) produce no significant effects across any analysis. Fine-tuned RoBERTa's only significant finding is an isolated Hillary Clinton result in the multi-treatment analysis. Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini finds Politics significant. The direction of the Trump sentiment effect reverses between paradigms: negative under Human, Fine-tuned RoBERTa, and GPT-4o-mini (baseline), but positive under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini.

At the \textbf{community level}, a clearer pattern emerges. The Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini model is the only annotator to produce significant community-level effects (Communities 3, 9, and 11), with the baseline GPT-4o-mini showing a single significant effect (Community 1). Human and Llama-3.3-70B annotations remain null at both levels. Llama-3.3-70B consistently compresses effect sizes toward zero across all communities, suggesting a general attenuation bias distinct from the GPT models' behavior. The community-level mediation analysis ($9 \to 11$) reveals a significant direct effect exclusively under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini annotations (TE $= -0.108$, NDE $= -0.105$, 3.2\% mediated).

\subsection{The Shortcut Learning Interpretation}
\label{sec:shortcut-learning}

The pattern of results is consistent with shortcut learning \citep{yuan2024llmsovercomeshortcutlearning, zhou2024explorespuriouscorrelationsconcept}: fine-tuning on AllSidesV2 ideology labels exposes the model to a training distribution where positive Social Issues framing is statistically correlated with leftward labels. The model learns to use this framing pattern as a shortcut, producing correct labels within distribution while encoding a direct coupling undetected in human annotations. As \citet{Ly2024shortcutlearningmedicalAI} show in the medical AI domain, models optimized for in-distribution accuracy can internalize hidden spurious associations that only surface under distributional shift or causal probing. Our mediation analysis serves precisely this diagnostic function.

Three lines of evidence support this interpretation:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini achieves the highest classification accuracy (F1 = 72.48) but is the only annotator producing significant causal effects.} Higher classification accuracy does not guarantee preservation of causal structure, a finding structurally invisible to output-level evaluation metrics.

    \item \textbf{Baseline GPT-4o-mini and Llama-3.3-70B produce markedly different causal profiles without fine-tuning.} Baseline GPT-4o-mini defaults to a moderate prediction strategy with a centrist tendency (Figure~\ref{fig:bias_overall_appendix}), producing a significant effect only for Community~1 (Electoral Politics). Llama-3.3-70B produces null results across all communities despite a leftward prediction skew. Neither has learned the Social Issues shortcut. The divergence between baseline and fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini is therefore attributable entirely to the fine-tuning process.

    \item \textbf{The Community $9 \to 11$ mediation reveals a near-total direct effect.} Under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini, the NDE accounts for 96.8\% of the total effect ($-0.105$ of $-0.108$), indicating that sentiment toward Geopolitics and Foreign Affairs directly predicts ideology classification without routing through Trump Presidency sentiment. This is the signature of a learned shortcut: the model has encoded a direct mapping from community sentiment to ideology that bypasses the expected mediating pathways.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Consolidated Summary}
\label{sec:causal-summary}

We conducted topic-level and community-level causal inference analyses across five annotation paradigms applied to \num{11516} news articles from the AllSides dataset. The principal findings are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{No robust topic-level causal effects.} Across mediation and multi-treatment analyses, no topic sentiment produces a statistically significant effect consistent across annotation paradigms. Human and GPT-4o-mini (baseline) find no significant effects in any analysis; Fine-tuned RoBERTa's sole significant result (Hillary Clinton in the multi-treatment analysis) does not replicate under any other paradigm.

  \item \textbf{Community-level aggregation reveals paradigm-specific effects.} Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini produces significant ATEs for Communities 3 (Social Issues), 9 (Geopolitics), and 11 (Trump Presidency). Baseline GPT-4o-mini is significant only for Community~1 (Electoral Politics). Human, Fine-tuned RoBERTa, and Llama-3.3-70B annotations are null throughout.

  \item \textbf{Sign reversals between human and LLM annotations.} In Communities 3 and 11, the estimated causal direction reverses entirely between human and Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini annotations, indicating qualitatively different sentiment-ideology mappings.

  \item \textbf{Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini exhibits shortcut learning.} Despite achieving the highest F1 score, the fine-tuned model is the only annotator producing significant community-level causal effects and mediation pathways, consistent with spurious sentiment-ideology coupling acquired during fine-tuning.

  \item \textbf{The Community $9 \to 11$ mediation effect is almost entirely direct.} Under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini, 96.8\% of the total effect operates through a direct pathway, with negligible mediation through Community~11 sentiment.

  \item \textbf{The reverse pathway ($11 \to 9$) is null for all annotators.} The asymmetry between $9 \to 11$ (significant) and $11 \to 9$ (null) reflects the concentrated nature of Community~9's ideological signal versus the diffuse influence of Community~11.

  \item \textbf{Llama-3.3-70B exhibits systematic attenuation.} Despite its pronounced leftward prediction distribution, Llama-3.3-70B produces null findings across all community-level analyses, consistently compressing effect sizes toward zero. This attenuation pattern, distinct from the GPT models' sign reversals, may reflect more conservative sentiment predictions and its lower classification accuracy (F1 $= 54.61$).

  \item \textbf{No CATE heterogeneity by outlet ideology.} Within each paradigm, the estimated topic-level causal effect of sentiment on ideology is uniform across Left, Centre, and Right outlet groups.

  \item \textbf{Annotation methodology can reverse estimated causal directions.} The Trump sentiment effect reverses sign between paradigms at the topic level, and community-level sign reversals occur for Communities 3 and 11, underscoring the importance of annotation-model robustness checks in computational social science.
\end{enumerate}
