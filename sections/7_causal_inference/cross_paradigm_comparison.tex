\section{Cross-Paradigm Synthesis}
\label{sec:cross-paradigm-synthesis}

Having presented community-level and topic-level results separately, we now synthesize findings across both analysis levels and all five annotation paradigms.

\subsection{Agreement Patterns Across Analysis Levels}

At the \textbf{community level}, two cells reach significance across the five paradigms. Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini produces a single significant ATE in Community~3 (Social Issues, ATE $= +0.027$), and baseline GPT-4o-mini produces a single significant ATE in Community~9 (Geopolitics, ATE $= -0.015$). The remaining three annotation paradigms (Human, Fine-tuned RoBERTa, and Llama-3.3-70B) produce no significant community-level ATEs. In most communities, the five paradigms agree on the direction of the sentiment-ideology effect; the differences are primarily in magnitude. Llama-3.3-70B's effects are smaller than Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini's on broadly distributed communities (e.g., Communities~1 and~5) but comparable or larger on Communities~9 and~11; the earlier framing of Llama as a uniform attenuator no longer holds. The community-level mediation analyses produce two significant direct effects: baseline GPT-4o-mini's NDE in $9 \to 11$ ($-0.093$, 95\% CI $[-0.174, -0.002]$) and Llama-3.3-70B's NDE in $11 \to 9$ ($-0.057$, 95\% CI $[-0.126, -0.003]$). No paradigm produces a significant Total Effect in either pathway.

At the \textbf{topic level}, the Donald Trump mediation analysis produces a single significant Total Effect under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini (TE $= -0.249$, 95\% CI $[-0.465, -0.033]$); all five paradigms agree on a negative direction in this analysis, and Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini's TE is roughly $3.2\times$ the magnitude of the zero-shot GPT-4o-mini baseline ($-0.078$) on the same article subset. In the multi-treatment continuous analysis, no topic sentiment produces a statistically significant effect that is consistent across more than one annotation paradigm: Fine-tuned RoBERTa's isolated Hillary Clinton result is the only significant cell. Human, GPT-4o-mini (baseline), and Llama-3.3-70B annotations produce no significant topic-level effects across the multi-treatment analysis.
