\section{Cross-Paradigm Synthesis}
\label{sec:cross-paradigm-synthesis}

Having presented community-level and topic-level results separately, we now synthesize findings across both analysis levels and all five annotation paradigms.

\subsection{Agreement Patterns Across Analysis Levels}

At the \textbf{community level}, a clear pattern emerges: the Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini model is the only annotator to produce significant community-level effects (Communities 3, 9, and 11), with the baseline GPT-4o-mini showing a single significant effect (Community 1). Human, Fine-tuned RoBERTa, and Llama-3.3-70B annotations remain null at both levels. Llama-3.3-70B consistently compresses effect sizes toward zero across all communities, suggesting a general attenuation bias distinct from the GPT models' behavior. The community-level mediation analysis ($9 \to 11$) reveals a significant direct effect exclusively under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini annotations (TE $= -0.108$, NDE $= -0.105$, 3.2\% mediated).

At the \textbf{topic level}, no topic sentiment produces a statistically significant effect that is consistent across annotation paradigms. Human and GPT-4o-mini (baseline) produce no significant effects across any analysis. Fine-tuned RoBERTa's only significant finding is an isolated Hillary Clinton result in the multi-treatment analysis. Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini finds Politics significant. The direction of the Trump sentiment effect reverses between paradigms: negative under Human, Fine-tuned RoBERTa, and GPT-4o-mini (baseline), but positive under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini.

\subsection{The Shortcut Learning Interpretation}
\label{sec:shortcut-learning}

The pattern of results is consistent with shortcut learning \citep{yuan2024llmsovercomeshortcutlearning, zhou2024explorespuriouscorrelationsconcept}: fine-tuning on AllSidesV2 ideology labels exposes the model to a training distribution where positive Social Issues framing is statistically correlated with leftward labels. The model learns to use this framing pattern as a shortcut, producing correct labels within distribution while encoding a direct coupling undetected in human annotations. As \citet{Ly2024shortcutlearningmedicalAI} show in the medical AI domain, models optimized for in-distribution accuracy can internalize hidden spurious associations that only surface under distributional shift or causal probing. Our mediation analysis serves precisely this diagnostic function.

Three lines of evidence support this interpretation. Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini achieves the highest classification accuracy (F1 = 72.48) yet is the only annotator producing significant causal effects---higher classification accuracy does not guarantee preservation of causal structure, a finding structurally invisible to output-level evaluation metrics. Baseline GPT-4o-mini and Llama-3.3-70B produce markedly different causal profiles without fine-tuning: baseline GPT-4o-mini defaults to a moderate prediction strategy with a centrist tendency, producing a significant effect only for Community~1 (Electoral Politics), while Llama-3.3-70B produces null results across all communities despite a leftward prediction skew; neither has learned the Social Issues shortcut, so the divergence between baseline and fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini is attributable entirely to the fine-tuning process. The Community $9 \to 11$ mediation reveals a near-total direct effect: under Fine-tuned GPT-4o-mini, the NDE accounts for 96.8\% of the total effect ($-0.105$ of $-0.108$), indicating that sentiment toward Geopolitics and Foreign Affairs directly predicts ideology classification without routing through Trump Presidency sentiment---the signature of a learned shortcut where the model has encoded a direct mapping from community sentiment to ideology that bypasses the expected mediating pathways.
