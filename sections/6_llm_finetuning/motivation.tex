\section{Motivation and Research Questions}
\label{sec:llm-motivation}

While our baseline experiments (Chapter~\ref{sec:baselines}) demonstrated that zero-shot large language models achieve competitive but suboptimal performance compared to domain-adapted transformers, the potential for task-specific fine-tuning to close this performance gap remains underexplored. The superior performance of the specialized POLITICS model over general-purpose LLMs raises several important questions about data efficiency (how much task-specific training data is required for LLMs to achieve competitive performance with domain-adapted transformers), input length sensitivity (whether LLMs exhibit different performance characteristics when fine-tuned on truncated versus full-length news articles), and scalability properties (the relationship between training data volume and classification performance for political bias detection). These questions are particularly relevant given the substantial computational and economic costs associated with LLM fine-tuning, necessitating careful analysis of cost-benefit trade-offs in practical deployment scenarios.
