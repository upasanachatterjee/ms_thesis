\section{Metalinguistic Feature Integration: Empirical Findings and Theoretical Implications}
\label{sec:metalinguistic-analysis}

The continued pre-training results in Section~\ref{sec:continued-pretraining-results} establish that metalinguistic auxiliary objectives can improve article-level ideology classification, but only under specific configurations. We focus here on the broader implications of those findings rather than re-stating the per-configuration F1 numbers.

Three patterns from Chapter~\ref{sec:continued-pretraining} are worth foregrounding. First, theme prediction is the auxiliary objective that delivers the largest and most consistent gains, particularly for the historically difficult center and right categories. This is consistent with the cognitive-science framing that topical emphasis is a structural feature of how outlets encode stance, complementing rather than substituting for the lexical content captured by contrastive pre-training. Second, the benefit of auxiliary objectives is contingent on having a sufficiently dense primary contrastive signal: with sparse triplet supervision, the auxiliary heads inject more noise than signal. This suggests that multi-task pre-training is best viewed not as a way to compensate for weak contrastive supervision, but as a way to amplify a sufficiently strong one. Third, the gains do not stack additively: combining theme and tone often underperforms theme alone, indicating that the two metalinguistic dimensions overlap in the discriminative information they contribute and that naive summation of auxiliary losses is not the right integration strategy.

Taken together, these results validate the central premise of the thesis, namely that metalinguistic structure carries ideological signal that is not fully captured by a content-only contrastive objective, while also identifying clear optimization-side preconditions on when that signal can be exploited. The practical implication for future continued pre-training work is that auxiliary metalinguistic supervision is worth including, but only after the primary objective and its supervision density have been tuned.
