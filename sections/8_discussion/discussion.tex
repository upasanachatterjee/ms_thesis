\chapter{Discussion and Analysis}
\label{ch:discussion}

This chapter provides an analysis of our experimental findings from the metalinguistic feature integration framework (Chapter~\ref{sec:continued-pretraining}), the large language model fine-tuning investigation (Chapter~\ref{sec:llm-finetuning}), and the causal inference analysis (Chapter~\ref{ch:causal-inference}). We examine the theoretical implications, practical trade-offs, and methodological insights emerging from our comparative evaluation of specialized transformer architectures versus general-purpose LLMs for political bias classification, as well as the implications of causal divergences between human and LLM annotations. Our discussion synthesizes empirical observations with broader considerations of computational efficiency, interpretability, deployment viability, and annotation trustworthiness.
